Mother Teresa

The nun dedicated to helping the poor

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1910 - Born in Skopje, North Macedonia
  • 1928 - Joins the Sisters of Loreto at Loreto Abbey in Rathfarnham, Ireland, to learn English with the intent of becoming a missionary.
  • 1929 - Arrives in India and and began her novitiate in Darjeeling, in the lower Himalayas,where she learned Bengali and taught at St. Teresa's School near her convent.
  • 1931 - Takes her first religious vows on 24 May 1931 and choses to be named after Thérèse de Lisieux, the patron saint of missionaries.
  • 1950 - Founds the Missionaries of Charity, choosing a white sari with two blue borders as the order's habit.
  • 1952 - Opens her first hospice with help from Calcutta officials. She converted an abandoned Hindu temple into the Kalighat Home for the Dying, free for the poor, and renamed it Kalighat, the Home of the Pure Heart
  • 1960s - Opens hospices, orphanages and leper houses throughout India.
  • 1965 - Expands congregation abroad with houses in Venezuela and in Italy (Rome).
  • 1979 - Receives the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1980 - Receives the Bharat Ratna (India's highest civilian award).
  • 1981 - Founds the Corpus Christi Movement for Priests and with Joseph Langford founded the Missionaries of Charity Fathers to combine the vocational aims of the Missionaries of Charity with the resources of the priesthood
  • 1982 - Rescues 37 children trapped in a front-line hospital by brokering a temporary cease-fire between the Israeli army and Palestinian guerrillas. Accompanied by Red Cross workers, she travelled through the war zone to the hospital to evacuate the young patients.
  • 1997 - Dies at the age of 87.
